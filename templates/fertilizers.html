Fertilizers

Urea is a nitrogen fertilizer and is often used for rice cultivation. It provides a quick release of nitrogen, promoting vegetative growth.

DAP is a source of phosphorus and nitrogen. Phosphorus is crucial for root development and early growth stages of rice.

Potassium is essential for overall plant health and plays a role in the development of rice grains. Potassium chloride is a common source of potassium

Ammonium sulfate is a quick-release nitrogen fertilizer. It also provides sulfur, which is beneficial for rice growth.

Similar to potassium chloride, muriate of potash is a source of potassium. It is important for grain filling and overall plant health

Depending on soil test results, micronutrient fertilizers containing elements like zinc, iron, manganese, and copper may be applied to address specific deficiencies

MAP is another phosphorus fertilizer option that provides both phosphorus and nitrogen.

Sulfur is an essential nutrient for maize growth. Fertilizers containing sulfur, such as ammonium sulfate (21-0-0), can be used to supplement sulfur requirements.

These fertilizers provide a balanced mix of nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), supporting overall growth and development

Rock phosphate is a slow-release source of phosphorus, supporting root development and flowering

Magnesium is a vital nutrient for coconut trees. Epsom salt can be used as a source of magnesium and sulfur

These fertilizers provide a mix of nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), supporting overall growth, flowering, and fruit development in orange trees

Phosphorus is crucial for root development and flowering. Super phosphate fertilizers can provide a readily available source of phosphorus

These formulations are designed specifically for citrus trees, including oranges. They often contain additional micro-nutrients like iron, zinc, manganese, and copper, which are important for citrus health

Lime (calcium carbonate) or dolomite can be added to address calcium and magnesium deficiencies if present in the soil

In addition to soil application, foliar sprays containing essential micro-nutrients can be applied directly to the leaves for better nutrient absorption

These materials contribute to soil structure, improve moisture retention, and provide a slow release of nutrients. Organic amendments also support microbial activity in the soil
